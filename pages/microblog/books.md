# Books

## The View from Nowhere, Thomas Nagle (02 JUL -)

Written in the pedantic style of old philosophers. Nagle makes a very big deal about the separation of objective and subjective reality. Basically qualia. He says things that I find silly, like that "being square" is objective, but "being red" is subjective because of how humans percieve colors. I don't get it. From a pure physicalist/realist/reductionist point of view (me!) this is just human sensors diserning a frequency band of electromagnetic radiation. Big deal. "The fealing of red" may be a qualia thing and you may be skeptical of assuming that other humans experience it like you do, etc. But red is just what we humans call a 620-750nm wavelength. What's the big deal?
